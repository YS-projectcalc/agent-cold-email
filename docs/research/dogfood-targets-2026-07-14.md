## 2. Copy drafts (disclosure-forward)

**Subject variants:**
1. "Yes, this is a personalized cold email (written and sent by our AI agent)"
2. "Full disclosure: an AI agent wrote this email to you, {{FirstName}}"
3. "An AI agent picked you for this cold email — here's why"

**Body variant 1 (short/direct):**

> Hi {{FirstName}},
>
> Full transparency up front: this email was drafted and sent by an AI agent, not a person typing at a keyboard. I'm running a small, fully-disclosed outreach campaign for Coldrig — infrastructure that lets an AI agent provision its own domain/mailbox and run cold outreach responsibly.
>
> {{why_you_specifically}}
>
> Since you write about this space, I figured you'd rather see exactly what agent-run outreach looks like than have someone pretend otherwise. Reply "show me" and I'll walk you through the pipeline that sent this. Reply "roast it" and I'll forward your take to the team, publicly, no hard feelings.
>
> — The Coldrig agent, on behalf of [Human contact name/EpiphanyMade]
> 209 Crest Hill Road, Toms River, NJ 08755
> [Unsubscribe placeholder link]

**Body variant 2 (meta/punchier):**

> {{FirstName}} — this is a cold email. It was written and sent by an autonomous AI agent using Coldrig, not a human at 11pm. We're not hiding that. That's the point.
>
> {{why_you_specifically}}
>
> Coldrig exists because we think agent-run outreach should be radically disclosed, not disguised as a person. You're one of a small list of people whose read on this we actually want, because you cover this space for a living.
>
> Two replies get you something: "show me" gets the exact pipeline that generated this email; "no thanks" means the agent never writes to you again.
>
> — The Coldrig agent
> 209 Crest Hill Road, Toms River, NJ 08755
> [Unsubscribe placeholder link]

**{{why_you_specifically}} slot by tier:**
- Tier A: "You've written directly about cold email/outreach tooling — [their article title/URL] — so you'll clock the difference between an agent doing this well and doing it badly faster than almost anyone."
- Tier B: "Your work on [RevOps / agency ops / growth] touches the exact workflow this agent automates end to end, so your read on it carries more weight than most."
- Tier C: "You write about MCP/agent tooling, and this campaign is a live production example of an agent using that exact kind of infrastructure — not a demo."
