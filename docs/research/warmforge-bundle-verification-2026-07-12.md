# Salesforge→Warmforge Bundle — Desk Verification (FROZEN, 2026-07-12)

> Frozen research record. Public-record-only pass (ToS/pricing/help/whitelabel pages, all fetched 2026-07-12); no signups, no vendor contact. Resolves the ACTIVATION Gate-1 item "verify the Salesforge→Warmforge unlimited-warmup bundle at reseller scale" to the extent the public record allows.

## VERDICT: BUNDLE LIMITED — do not build the pricing model on it

Three compounding primary-source constraints:

1. **ToS Article 3.5 (Fair Usage, salesforge.ai/legal/terms; mirrored near-verbatim at warmforge.ai/terms):** Salesforge "may remove accounts from the email warmup service if they pose performance risks" and **"might limit maximum of 99 connected email accounts per workspace at any given time."** A discretionary per-workspace cap aimed specifically at warmup — directly contradicting the pricing page's unqualified "Unlimited Premium Warm Up" (both Pro $40 and Growth $80 carry identical language, no fine print on the pricing page itself).
2. **ToS Article 3.1:** assigning use of the Services to your own customers is prohibited unless formally enrolled as an "indirect subscriber" with mandatory sub-accounts (same reseller framework as Mailforge's ToS).
3. **The decisive fact — salesforge.ai/whitelabel FAQ, verbatim:** **"At the moment Warmforge is not included in the whitelabelling option, however this can change in the future."** Salesforge's own official multi-client reseller product explicitly EXCLUDES Warmforge today.

**Residual open question (mechanical):** whether a mailbox can draw bundled warmup while sending exclusively through a non-Salesforge engine (warm-only connection), and whether warm-only connections count against the 99-cap. help.salesforge.ai says Warmforge "operates independently" from sending (decoupling article) but nothing states warm-only eligibility for the FREE bundle. A ready-to-send 3-question support inquiry is drafted below — optional upside, not a blocker.

**Also noted [secondary]:** one 2026 review reports Warmforge operational-reliability complaints (mailboxes disconnecting, DNS-tracking failures). No public reports of the 99-cap being enforced (absence ≠ evidence).

## Pricing implication — the RAMP-ONLY model replaces the bundle as the margin basis

With the bundle unreliable, the Mailforge-path warmup cost basis becomes **ramp-only standalone Warmforge slots** (no Salesforge dependency, $10/slot/mo, first slot free): warmup is front-loaded (~first 3–4 weeks per mailbox — SPEC §9), so the warmup fleet is sized to the ONBOARDING RATE, not the total mailbox base, and slots rotate across cohorts. Illustration: 200 active mailboxes onboarding ~20–30/mo → ~$200–300/mo warmup → **~$1–1.50/active-mailbox amortized**. Revised Mailforge-path all-in: $3.00 slot + ~$1–1.50 ramp warmup + ~$0.50 domains ≈ **$4.50–5.00/mbx** → the $13–15 retail line clears **~2.6–3.3×**. The margin model survives without the bundle. (Even the 99-cap is a non-issue at ramp-only volumes for early scale.) Longer-term: internal tenant-pool warmup at critical mass (ROADMAP 2026-07-12 "warmup COGS strategy ladder") removes the vendor line entirely.
