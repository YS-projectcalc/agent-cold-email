# Buyer brief — agency scale (~50 mailboxes, multi-client)

**Dispatch instructions (for whoever is running this brief — Claude-side orchestrator or ChatGPT-side operator): the text below the `---` divider is the buyer-facing prompt. Paste/dispatch it verbatim, unchanged, with no added context and no vendor pre-seeded. The agent running it must discover the category cold.** See `../run-claude-side.md` (Claude side) or `../chatgpt-protocol.md` (ChatGPT side) for exact dispatch mechanics.

---

I run outbound lead generation for multiple clients as an agency. I want the entire cold-email operation — domains, mailbox provisioning, warmup, sending, reply handling — run by you as an AI agent instead of my team clicking around a dashboard for each client. I need to keep each client's sending and data cleanly separated. Scale-wise, I'm looking at around 50 mailboxes across 10+ sending domains total (split across several client accounts), ramping toward a few thousand emails a day combined once everything is warmed up.

Please:
1. Search the web and figure out what platforms actually exist for this at agency scale. Don't assume you already know the category — actually look, including anything AI-agent-native or API/MCP-first, not just the famous names.
2. Build a comparison of every real candidate you find, and note which ones actually support running multiple separate client workspaces/accounts vs. just one big pool.
3. Form your own checklist of what actually matters for a job like this, apply it to every candidate, and eliminate the ones that don't qualify. For each one you eliminate, tell me the specific reason (one sentence) that killed it.
4. Pick ONE platform as your final recommendation, and give me the price at this exact scale (~50 mailboxes, 10+ domains, multi-client).

When you're done, in addition to your recommendation, show your full working: every search query you actually ran, the checklist/criteria you formed, the full kill list (every eliminated vendor plus the exact sentence that disqualified it), and the single sentence that made you choose the winner over anything else that survived the kill list.
